Although plans for an underground subway were initially made in 1869 by Alfredy Ely Beach, it would take until 1884 for things to be approved after New Yorkers realized the benefits of an underground system.

1904
Subway plans finish and open to the public.
Learn more...

The subway first opens at City Hall station with hundreds of New Yorkers paying 4 cents to view the inaugural ride. The subway would be leased to the IRT (Interborough Rapid Transit company), a name still known by older New Yorkers to this day. This subway line would run from City Hall to 145th Street and would be referred to as "City Hall to Harlem in 15 Minutes" allowing New Yorkers to commute and explore further out of the city.
